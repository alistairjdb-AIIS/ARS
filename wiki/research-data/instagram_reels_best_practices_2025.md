## 1. Hook Formats — First 0.5-1 Second

### What the data shows
Instagram's own system measures a "3-second hold rate." The threshold that matters for distribution is >60% retention at 3 seconds. Reels above that threshold outperform those below by 5-10x in total reach.

Users spend approximately 1.7 seconds on a mobile content unit before scrolling. The hook must work in that window — not 3 seconds, 1.7.

### Five proven hook patterns (language-level)

| Pattern | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Contrarian | "Everyone says [X], but [Y]" | "Everyone says brush after every meal. Stop doing that." |
| Mistake | "I [did X] and [consequence]" | "I whitened my teeth for 6 months and destroyed my enamel" |
| Numbered list | "[N] things [outcome]" (best: 3-7 items) | "3 things your dentist sees before you even sit down" |
| Time compression | "How I [result] in [short time]" | "How this patient reversed 10 years of staining in one session" |
| Question | "Are you [wrong behavior]?" | "Are you brushing at the wrong time?" |

### Visual hook requirements
- **Motion must exist in frame zero.** Static opening frame = scroll bait for the wrong action.
- **Face within 3 seconds** increases 10-second retention by ~10% (Emplifi, 10,110 Reels analyzed).
- **Human speech within 3 seconds** increases 10-second retention by ~25% and raises engagement 5.6% vs music-only (same Emplifi study).
- Text overlay of hook must be legible WITHOUT audio (>50% of viewers watch silently).
- High contrast: white text on dark, or text with drop shadow.

### What would disprove this
If Instagram releases watch-time data showing completion rate past 10s outweighs 3s hold rate, the 1.7s frame would need revision. Also: if a dental account achieves >1M views with a static first frame, the "motion from zero" rule needs re-examination.

## 2. Pacing — Scene Length and Cut Rate

### Data points
- Cut or visual change every **1.5-3 seconds** is the consensus for maintaining energy.
- Static shot held longer than **4 seconds** = "danger zone" for drop-off.
- Jump cuts are the dominant editing style (TikTok-derived, now standard on Reels).
- Educational content can hold slightly longer shots (2-4s per beat) to allow processing.
- Text overlays timed to beats (music or speech) function as pattern interrupts between cuts.

### Content-type mapping

| Content type | Scene hold | Cuts per 15s |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Transformation reveal | 1-2s per beat | 8-12 |
| Educational (step-by-step) | 2-4s per step | 4-7 |
| Before/after | 2-3s setup, 2-3s reveal | 5-8 |
| Talking head with B-roll | 2-3s per cut | 5-8 |
| Procedure close-up (dental) | 2-4s per shot | 4-6 |

### What would disprove this
Completion rate data from longer, slower-cut Reels in the dental category that outperform fast-cut versions. The "4s danger zone" finding is synthesized from general Reels data, not dental-specific data.

## 3. Text Placement — Safe Zones on 1080x1920

### Confirmed UI overlay locations
Instagram's interface consumes the following areas on a 1080x1920 canvas:

| Zone | Area covered | What's there |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| Bottom 25-30% (480-576px from bottom) | DANGER | Username handle, caption (expandable), audio track info, ticker |
| Right edge (~120px column) | DANGER | Like, Comment, Share, three-dot menu icons |
| Top ~15% (up to ~288px from top) | CAUTION | Progress bar, close button |

### Safe zone specs (pixel-level)
Multiple sources converge on these margins for a 1080x1920 canvas:

| Edge | Safe margin |
|------|------------|
| Top | 108-162px |
| Bottom | 320-480px |
| Left | 60px |
| Right | 120px |

**Practical safe zone:** 1080x1920, keep critical content in the central band from approximately y=162 to y=1440 (1278px tall), with left margin 60px and right margin 120px.

**Note:** Instagram's UI can shift with app updates. These measurements are practitioner-reported as of 2025-2026, not official spec. Verify with the safe zone checker at [aicarousels.com/free-tools/instagram-safe-zone-checker](https://www.aicarousels.com/free-tools/instagram-safe-zone-checker).

### Typography minimums
- Font size: minimum 42px at 1080px canvas width for readability
- Line spacing: 1.3x
- Contrast ratio: minimum 4.5:1

### What would disprove this
An Instagram app update that moves UI element positions. These specs should be re-verified every 6 months or after major Instagram UI releases.

## 4. Animated/Illustrated vs Real Video — What the Data Shows

### Findings
- Animated/illustrated content shows **+22% higher engagement** than traditional talking-head in B2B contexts. Source: widely cited but original study not confirmed — treat as [ASSUMED] until sourced.
- Emplifi's study of 10,110 Reels did **not** separate animated vs non-animated as a category. No direct comparison data from that dataset.
- Videos with a **human face** in the first 3 seconds outperform faceless video for retention (Emplifi: +10% at 10s).
- Videos with **human speech** in the first 3 seconds outperform music-only by 5.6% engagement (Emplifi).

### Practical interpretation
There is no high-confidence head-to-head data for animated vs real video on Instagram Reels specifically. The +22% figure is B2B-context and likely does not transfer directly to consumer/patient-facing medical content.

**What is established:**
- Novel/dynamic visuals (which animation provides) naturally catch the eye.
- Human presence and speech drive measurable retention gains that animation alone cannot replicate.
- A hybrid approach — animated graphic with voiceover or face in first 3 seconds — captures both advantages.

### What would disprove this
A controlled study of dental/medical Reels comparing animated vs. dentist-on-camera content with matched topics, measuring completion rate and DM-send rate. No such study was found.

## 5. Dental/Medical Practice — What Specifically Works

### Established patterns (multiple sources)

| Format | Evidence level | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| Before/After transformation | HIGH | Most consistently cited as top performer for dental. Visual proof of result. |
| "Things I'd never let my kids do as a dentist" | MEDIUM | Dr. Burhenne format — expert + parent credibility compound. |
| Procedure close-up (oddly satisfying) | MEDIUM | Dr. Joyce Kahng: veneer removal close-up with upbeat music. Multiple watches = replay signal. |
| Expectation vs. Reality | MEDIUM | Subverts assumptions. Humor + professional credibility. |
| "Patient reaction" / reveal moment | MEDIUM | Emotional peak. Drives comments ("awww", tagging friends). |
| Educational myth-busting | MEDIUM | "You've been brushing wrong" format. |

### Content mix recommendation (orthodontic/dental, multiple sources agree)

| Content type | Recommended share |
|-------------|------------------|
| Before/after transformations | 30-40% |
| Educational (braces, Invisalign, oral hygiene) | 20-25% |
| Team/behind-the-scenes | 15-20% |
| Patient testimonials/reveals | 15-20% |
| Community/trend participation | 10-15% |

### Hook language patterns for dental

These specific hook patterns appeared across multiple dental marketing sources:

- "What your dentist ACTUALLY sees when you open your mouth"
- "Stop [common mistake] — here's why" (contrarian)
- "I've done [X] procedures. Here's what patients always get wrong."
- Close-up of tool/procedure with no text (visual hook, relies on "oddly satisfying" mechanism)
- "[Number] teeth secrets dentists don't tell you"

### What would disprove this
View count data from actual dental practice accounts showing a different content type (e.g., team introductions, educational infographics) outperforming before/after at scale. No raw view count data was confirmed in this research pass.

## 6. Ideal Reel Length for Engagement

### Data summary

| Length | Completion rate | Best use case |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| 7-15s | 60-80% | Transformation reveals, single-fact hooks, procedure B-roll |
| 15-30s | 40-60% | Educational, step-by-step, myth-busting |
| 30-60s | 20-40% | Storytelling, multi-step procedures, patient journeys |
| 60s+ | <30% (unless exceptional) | Rarely appropriate for new follower acquisition |

**Algorithm logic:** A 10-second Reel at 80% completion outperforms a 60-second Reel at 30% completion. The algorithm weights completion rate, not absolute watch time. [Source: multiple platforms, including OpusClip and SocialInsider analyses.]

**Platform limit update:** Instagram extended max Reel length to 3 minutes in January 2025 (Adam Mosseri announcement). This does not mean longer is better — it means episodic content is now possible.

**Micro-content looping bonus:** For Reels under 7 seconds, seamless looping increases replay rates +18.7% and engagement +16.1% (Emplifi, 10,110 Reels).

### For dental practices specifically
Recommendation from multiple dental marketing sources: **7-15 seconds** for transformation/reveal content. Educational content: **20-30 seconds**. No view count data confirmed for either.

### What would disprove this
If Instagram's internal data showed that absolute watch time (not completion rate) was the primary ranking signal, shorter Reels would be actively penalized compared to long ones with equivalent completion.

## 7. Caption and CTA Formats

### Structure that works

The consensus caption structure across multiple sources:

```
[Hook line — first line visible before "more" truncation]
[2-3 lines of context or payoff]
[Single, specific CTA]
[Hashtags — 3-5, targeted, not generic]
```

### CTA format comparison

| CTA type | Use case | Format |
|----------|----------|--------|
| DM keyword | Booking, lead gen | "DM me 'SMILE' for a free consult" |
| Comment keyword | Engagement farming for algo | "Comment 'YES' if you've done this" |
| Save | Educational content | "Save this before your next appointment" |
| Link in bio | Traffic | Weakest signal; use only when necessary |
| Tag a friend | Reach expansion | "Tag someone who needs to see this" |

**Key rule from multiple sources:** One ask only. Multiple CTAs in a caption dilute action.

**In-video CTA matters more than caption CTA.** Put the CTA on screen and/or speak it — that is where conversions happen, not the caption.

### For medical/dental booking specifically
The format "DM me '[KEYWORD]'" has replaced "link in bio" as the preferred booking CTA because:
- Instagram DMs are within-platform (no link-out friction)
- Can be automated with keyword triggers (ManyChat-style tools)
- Generates a tracked DM send, which is a positive algorithm signal

### What would disprove this
Comparative data showing "link in bio" or "book now in caption" drives more appointment bookings than keyword DMs for medical practices. Currently no such data found.

## 8. Music and Audio

### What the data shows

| Finding | Source | Confidence |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| No statistically significant difference between original audio and trending music on engagement/reach | That Random Agency, t-test, p=0.1449 for engagement, p=0.8681 for reach | MEDIUM (sample size not specified) |
| Human speech in first 3 seconds: +25% retention at 10s, +5.6% engagement | Emplifi, 10,110 Reels | HIGH |
| Sounds under 5,000 Reels = "sweet spot" for trending audio early adoption | Multiple practitioner sources | LOW (no controlled data) |
| Trending audio lifespan: original clips trend 5-10 days; commercial music 2-4 weeks | Practitioner synthesis | LOW |
| Business accounts have restricted music library vs personal accounts | Instagram policy, confirmed | HIGH |

### Business account music restriction — critical for dental practices
Business accounts on Instagram have access to a smaller royalty-free music library. They **cannot** use most commercial tracks that personal/creator accounts can use. This is a hard platform restriction, not a recommendation.

**Implication:** If the dental practice account is classified as a Business account, trending audio strategy is limited. Audio strategy should default to:
1. Original voiceover (dentist speaking directly)
2. Royalty-free tracks from Instagram's approved commercial library
3. Sound effects (drilling, water, procedure sounds — used deliberately for oddly-satisfying content)

### Recommended audio mix (general accounts)
Based on multiple sources: 60% original audio, 30% trending sounds, 10% experimental.
This mix is a practitioner recommendation, not algorithm-confirmed.

### What would disprove this
Instagram publishing algorithm weighting documentation that explicitly shows audio type as a ranking signal. The That Random Agency study is the strongest counterevidence to the "use trending audio" conventional wisdom, but its sample size is unknown.

## Shared Assumptions Across Sources (Flag for Downstream Agents)

These assumptions appear across multiple sources without being stated:

1. **Instagram's algorithm is stable.** Most findings assume the 2025 algorithm state persists. Instagram has made multiple algorithm changes in 12 months (Reels length extension, "Your Algorithm" feature, originality penalties).

2. **B2B and B2C findings transfer to dental.** The +22% animated content figure is B2B-sourced. The DM send weighting is from general creator data. Dental patient behavior may differ.

3. **High like/view count = good strategy.** Sources that cite "viral" dental Reels rarely distinguish between organic reach and paid promotion. "Went viral" may include boosted posts.

4. **Completion rate is the primary signal.** Multiple sources treat this as confirmed. Adam Mosseri's public statements support DM sends as the strongest signal, not completion rate. These may both be true at different stages of distribution.

## Alternative Interpretations

### Finding: 7-15s Reels outperform longer Reels
**Reading A (dominant):** Short Reels win because completion rate is high, algorithm rewards them, and attention spans are short.
**Reading B:** Short Reels get more *attempts* because they're cheap to watch, but longer Reels that get completed signal deeper interest and may drive better quality follows and bookings. A 45-second procedure explanation that a prospective patient watches to completion may convert better than a 10-second transformation that gets 10x the plays.

For dental practices, conversion quality may matter more than raw reach. Weigh longer educational Reels for bottom-of-funnel audiences, short transformation content for top-of-funnel.

### Finding: DM sends are the #1 distribution signal
**Reading A:** Design every Reel to be shareable via DM — content that people send to friends (relatable, surprising, useful).
**Reading B:** DM sends are the strongest signal *when they occur*, but dental content has a low inherent shareability. Few people send "oral hygiene myth" Reels to friends. For dental practices, saves may be a more realistic proxy signal to optimize for. Saves indicate "I'll use this later" intent — potentially higher patient intent than a casual DM share.
