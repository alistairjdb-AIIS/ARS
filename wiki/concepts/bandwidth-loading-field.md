# Bandwidth, Loading, and Field: Three Meta-Patterns

> Three meta-patterns that emerge above the 10 generating principles: Bandwidth (no real transition is instant -- all must be distributed across time), Loading (value is created during preparation, not during display), and Field (direction, grouping, and attention are achieved by shaping the environment, not labeling the target). Together they organize ~100 design rules across 20 cross-domain dimensions.

**Confidence:** MEDIUM
**Last compiled:** 2026-04-06
**Sources:** 1 raw file, 0 memory files

## Core Findings

### Meta-Pattern 1: The Bandwidth Principle

**All real transitions must be distributed across time. Instant = infinite derivative = overload.** [THEORETICAL]

This principle unifies Entry/Arrival (P1), State Change (P4), Rhythm/Pacing (P5), and Departure/Exit (P8). The core insight from physics: a step function requires infinite bandwidth. In perceptual terms, an instant change overwhelms the processing system. Every domain encodes this: music has attack envelopes, architecture has threshold spaces, biology has sensory adaptation curves, linguistics has discourse markers.

#### Rules Under the Bandwidth Principle

**From Entry/Arrival (P1):**
- **Nucleation rule:** The first element contains the structural geometry of everything that follows. The seed must encode the whole. [THEORETICAL]
- **Amortization rule:** Information cost of novelty must be distributed across time. No instant introductions. [THEORETICAL]
- **Curve-not-step rule:** Every arrival is an easing curve, never a hard cut (unless the hard cut is the deliberate narrative beat). [THEORETICAL]

**From State Change (P4):**
- **Third-state rule:** Transitions are designed states, not gaps between states. Invest proportional design effort. [THEORETICAL]
- **The threshold is its own state:** 300-600ms where the system visibly works -- neither the old state nor the new state. [THEORETICAL]
- **Transformation rule:** The transition transforms the user's frame of reference, not just their location. [THEORETICAL]

**From Rhythm/Pacing (P5):**
- **Pink noise pacing:** Variation at all timescales simultaneously. 1/f distribution balances predictability and surprise. [THEORETICAL]
- **Three-suspension limit:** Never more than 3 unresolved conceptual threads active simultaneously. [THEORETICAL]
- **Entrain-violate-resolve:** Establish prediction, then break it, then resolve -- at micro, meso, and macro timescales. [THEORETICAL]

**From Departure/Exit (P8):**
- **Ritardando exit:** The last 15% of an exit takes 40%+ of total duration. Energy transfers gradually. [THEORETICAL]
- **Figure-dissolves-to-ground:** Exiting elements approach background properties before disappearing. [THEORETICAL]

### Meta-Pattern 2: The Loading Principle

**Value is created during preparation, not during display. Loading is not a cost -- it is where value is created.** [THEORETICAL]

This principle unifies Tension/Resolution (P10), Response/Feedback (P2), and Revelation (P3). The insight: the payoff's perceived value is proportional to the investment (temporal, cognitive, emotional) that preceded it. Minimizing preparation minimizes impact.

#### Rules Under the Loading Principle

**From Tension/Resolution (P10):**
- **Loading state as product:** Show calculation dimensions resolving, not a spinner. The loading experience IS the product at that moment. [THEORETICAL]
- **Proportional loading:** The depth and duration of loading should match the significance of the payoff. Trivial results need minimal loading; significant results deserve preparation. [THEORETICAL]
- **Anticipation architecture:** The moment before the reveal is the peak neurochemical moment (dopamine peaks in anticipation, not receipt). Design for that moment specifically. [VERIFIED -- dopamine anticipation research, Gruber et al. 2014]

**From Response/Feedback (P2):**
- **Back-channel signaling:** System says "uh-huh" during extended input. Intermediate acknowledgments during multi-step processes. [THEORETICAL]
- **Processing-depth encoding:** Response timing communicates how seriously the input was processed. Instant = trivial. Brief delay = computed. Long delay = deep analysis. [THEORETICAL]
- **Substrate rule:** Each interaction changes how the user sees, not just what they know. The response transforms the user's interpretive frame. [THEORETICAL]

**From Revelation (P3):**
- **Dependency-ordered disclosure:** Information revealed in order of interpretive dependency, not importance. Each element creates the context needed for the next. [THEORETICAL]
- **Frame-before-content:** Establish the interpretive frame, then deliver the content that inhabits it. [THEORETICAL]
- **Contour rule:** Rising sequences build anticipation; falling sequences deliver resolution. The shape of the sequence carries meaning independent of content. [THEORETICAL]

### Meta-Pattern 3: The Field Principle

**Direction, grouping, and attention are achieved by shaping the environment, not labeling the target.** [THEORETICAL]

This principle unifies Attention (P6), Grouping (P9), and Trust (P7). The insight: effective direction works like gravity, not like signage. You shape the field so the desired outcome is the point of lowest potential energy.

#### Rules Under the Field Principle

**From Attention (P6):**
- **Gradient surround:** De-emphasize surroundings; don't just brighten the target. The context creates the focus. [THEORETICAL]
- **Tilt-the-floor:** Shape the visual/cognitive field so the target is the point of natural convergence. [THEORETICAL]
- **Lowest-potential-energy rule:** Attention flows to the point where the perceptual field is most resolved. Design the field, not the pointer. [THEORETICAL]

**From Grouping (P9):**
- **Shared field grouping:** Same easing curve = same group. The gradient IS the container. No explicit borders needed. [THEORETICAL]
- **4x threshold:** Intra-group spacing must be 1/4 or less of inter-group spacing. The ratio, not the absolute distance, determines grouping. [THEORETICAL]
- **Three-zone system:** Intimate (closest), relational (mid), contextual (far). Three zones of proximity, each with different interaction rules. [THEORETICAL]
- **Edge-effect rule:** Boundaries are the highest-energy zones. The most meaningful events happen AT the boundary (hover states, transitions, thresholds). [THEORETICAL]

**From Trust (P7):**
- **Costly signal rule:** Trust requires signals that are expensive to fake. Cheap consistency signals (saying "we care") build zero trust. Costly consistency signals (actual behavior under pressure) build trust. [THEORETICAL]
- **Phase transition rule:** Trust does not accumulate linearly. It arrives suddenly once a threshold of accumulated evidence is crossed. [THEORETICAL]
- **Asymmetric response:** A single negative signal destroys more trust than a single positive signal builds. The negative-to-positive ratio is not 1:1. [THEORETICAL]

### The 10 Additional Sub-Dimensions (Time and Space)

Beyond the 10 original principles, 10 additional sub-dimensions were synthesized, organized into TIME (5) and SPACE (5). [THEORETICAL]

#### TIME Sub-Dimensions

**11. Duration:** Duration encodes value-weight as a ratio relative to a temporal baseline. It should emerge from content structure, not be imposed decoratively. [THEORETICAL]
- Duration scale: 100ms (micro-feedback) / 250ms (state change) / 500ms (navigation) / 1200ms (reveal) / 2500ms (ceremony)
- Result duration proportional to computational density
- Pause at transitions determines if the destination feels earned

**12. Sequence:** Sequence is a chain of context transformations. Each element delivers content AND transforms the interpretive frame for everything after it. [THEORETICAL]
- Frame before content (establish context, then deliver)
- Contour rule: rising = anticipation, falling = resolution
- Priority effect: first elements foreclose alternatives
- Middle deserves promotion or elimination (no throwaway middle elements)

**13. Simultaneity:** Simultaneity communicates STRUCTURE (relationship, comparison). Sequence communicates CAUSATION (narrative, transformation). [THEORETICAL]
- Commutativity test: if swapping order changes nothing, show simultaneously
- Niche differentiation: simultaneous elements must occupy different perceptual niches
- Binding principle: elements perceived as one unit must appear at the same time

**14. Memory/Anticipation:** The present is a membrane between compressed past and anticipated future. Meaning is the delta between what was carried forward and what is expected ahead. [THEORETICAL]
- Compress, don't replay (momentum, not event logs)
- Make future felt, not shown (dominant-seventh pull)
- Design entry to design arrival (compression before payoff)
- References point both ways (anaphora/cataphora)

**15. Tempo/Rate:** Systems respond to the derivative, not the state. 1st derivative captures attention. 2nd encodes emotion. 3rd triggers arousal. [THEORETICAL]
- Design acceleration curve, not duration
- Deceleration carries more information than acceleration
- The hold is the loudest moment (fermata principle)
- Match rate to emotional arc, not informational arc

#### SPACE Sub-Dimensions

**16. Proximity:** Distance encodes interaction strength via non-linear decay with categorical thresholds (not a gradient). [THEORETICAL]
- 4x threshold: intra-group distance is 1/4 of inter-group distance
- Three-zone system: intimate / relational / contextual
- Edge-effect: boundaries are highest-energy zones
- Metric-choice: visual, semantic, and temporal distance can conflict

**17. Depth:** Depth is a cognitive instruction, not a visual style. Each layer transforms conditions for layers below it. [THEORETICAL]
- Blur is reverb: 2-4px = near, 8-16px = mid, 20px+ = far (three distances)
- 4 depth planes max
- Background is an active participant, not passive scenery
- Depth transitions need directional logic

**18. Scale:** Size is not magnitude -- it is regime change. Crossing a size threshold changes which cognitive mode the viewer enters. [THEORETICAL]
- Three regimes: atmospheric (>50% viewport) / narrative (15-50%) / instrumental (<15%)
- Ratio ladder with consistent intervals
- Proportion shift as narrative event
- Viewport-to-element ratio = authority-to-autonomy ratio

**19. Direction:** Direction is the spatial encoding of effort (vertical), time (horizontal), and attention (depth). Never neutral. [THEORETICAL]
- Vertical: up = effort/aspiration, down = delivery/settling
- Horizontal: with reading flow = progress, against = reversal
- Depth: slow approach = invitation, fast = threat
- Results settle DOWN; errors move UP

**20. Enclosure:** Boundaries are zones with their own properties. The most meaningful events happen AT the boundary. [THEORETICAL]
- Borders are zones, not lines
- Control permeability, not opacity
- Boundaries generate their own content (hover states ARE ecotones)
- Boundaries don't recurse (no bordered cards inside bordered cards)
- Graduated enclosure = intimacy gradient

## Operational Rules

1. **When any element appears for the first time, apply the Bandwidth Principle** -- distribute its information cost across time with an easing curve. Never instant-on. Because the perceptual system requires ramp time to process novelty without overload. [THEORETICAL]

2. **When designing a loading or waiting state, apply the Loading Principle** -- make the waiting period itself productive by showing intermediate progress, dimensions resolving, or processing evidence. Because the payoff's perceived value is proportional to the loading that preceded it. [THEORETICAL]

3. **When directing user attention, apply the Field Principle** -- de-emphasize surroundings (gradient surround) rather than highlighting the target with labels or arrows. Because attention follows environmental gradients, not signposts. [THEORETICAL]

4. **When grouping related elements, use shared animation signature (same easing, same duration) as the container** -- because shared field participation communicates belonging more effectively than explicit borders. Maintain the 4x spacing ratio between intra-group and inter-group distances. [THEORETICAL]

5. **When designing exits or departures, apply the ritardando rule** -- the last 15% of the exit animation should take 40%+ of the total duration, and the element should approach its destination's properties (opacity, color, position) before disappearing. [THEORETICAL]

6. **When deciding between simultaneous and sequential presentation, apply the commutativity test** -- if swapping the order of two elements changes nothing about meaning, show them simultaneously (structure). If order matters, show them sequentially (causation). [THEORETICAL]

7. **When choosing animation direction, encode meaning** -- up = effort/aspiration, down = delivery/settling, with reading flow = progress, against = reversal, toward viewer = threat or urgency, away = departure. Direction is never neutral. [THEORETICAL]

8. **When managing cognitive load across a sequence, enforce the three-suspension limit** -- never more than 3 unresolved conceptual threads active at once. Each new thread must close a previous one or the viewer loses coherence. [THEORETICAL]

## Open Questions

- The "49 design rules" referenced in the source are summarized as top-10 actionable rules plus the three meta-patterns. The full derivation session (Mar 21, 2026) contains the complete set, but only the summary survived into the persistent research file. Some rules may be lost or under-documented.
- The time/space sub-dimensions (11-20) generate approximately 50 additional rules beyond the original 49, bringing the total to ~100. These have not been individually validated in design practice.
- Whether the 4x spacing threshold for grouping holds across all screen sizes and density contexts is untested. Mobile interfaces with constrained space may require different ratios.
- The three-suspension limit (never more than 3 unresolved threads) is stated without empirical citation. It may relate to working memory capacity research (Cowan's 4 +/- 1) but the connection is not explicitly made in the source.
